Preserving Structure

So far JsonTransform seemed quite similar to the JSONPath library, except the main difference in preserving the structure of returned results.

NOTE: if you want standard JSONPath behavior, you can use the f (flat) flag. For example you can try this two expressions: 

(f)$.a['aa', 'ab']['name', 'surname']
(f)$.a['aa', 'ab']['name', 'surname']; (f)$.krajina['name', 'surname']
You can also try to remove the flag, and understand the difference in behavior. F-flag also explains a potential of the JsonTransform lib in preserving the structure of results. However, we want more, we want to keep more of the structure, than the just ultimate result, we want to sometimes see the origine the result is coming from. Please check the exmample. As you can see, in this way we are able to understand that Nobel writer Ivo Andric, comes from serbia. and from more complex example
we can conclude that great Serbian scientist and inventer Nikola Tesla is coming from Krajina, a part of Austro-Hungary empire at that time (now part of Croatia)
